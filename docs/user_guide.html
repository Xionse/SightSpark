SightSpark User Guide

Welcome to the SightSpark user guide. This document is intended to provide information and resources for users who are interested in using the SightSpark software.

Getting started

To get started with using SightSpark, you will need to have the following:

  • A computer running Windows, Mac, or Linux operating system
  • The latest version of SightSpark software

You can download the latest version of the SightSpark software from the official website at [https://www.sightspark.com/download]

Once you have downloaded and installed the software, you can open it and begin using it with your preferred input method (keyboard, mouse, touchscreen, etc.)

Navigating the software

SightSpark has a variety of keyboard shortcuts and commands that you can use to navigate and interact with the software. You can find a full list of these shortcuts in the software's Help menu or on our website at [https://www.sightspark.com/help].

You can also use the software's settings menu to customize the software to suit your specific needs and preferences. For example, you can adjust the text-to-speech voice, the speech rate, and the volume.

Braille Support

SightSpark also supports braille displays, allowing you to read text on the screen in braille. You will need to connect a braille display to your computer and set it up in the software's settings.

For more information on setting up a braille display, please refer to the software's Help menu or visit our website at [https://www.sightspark.com/help/braille-display]

Support

If you need assistance with SightSpark, please visit our Support Center at [https://www.sightspark.com/support] for troubleshooting tips and FAQs. If you are unable to find the answer to your question, you can submit a support ticket for further assistance.

We also have a community forum on our website at [https://www.sightspark.com/community] where you can ask and answer questions, share tips and tricks, and connect with other SightSpark users.
